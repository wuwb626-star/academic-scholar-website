What I devote myself to

Remote sensing is changing the way we observe the world. Large-scale remote sensing provides insight into global changes, while fine-scale near-surface remote sensing reveals heterogeneity and dynamics of vegetation and ecosystems. Leveraging very-high-resolution satellites and UAV platforms, I aim to address key scientific questions in fine-scale vegetation monitoring and modeling:

  • How can existing remote sensing theories and methods be adapted for fine-scale observations, and how to tackle unique challenges in modeling and retrieval?
  • How to fully exploit multi-temporal, multi-modal observations to improve models and accuracy of vegetation variable retrieval?
  • How to apply remote sensing techniques in practical applications such as natural resource assessment, forest ecosystem evaluation, and crop monitoring?

Who I am

I am a remote sensing scientist integrating fine-scale UAV and very-high-resolution satellite observations with modeling and theory, aiming to better understand plants, vegetation communities, and ecosystems.

What I use

My research combines low-cost techniques (e.g., RGB cameras, photogrammetry) with advanced instruments (e.g., multispectral cameras, hyperspectral sensors, LiDAR). Radiative transfer theory and models (1D and 3D) underpin my work, alongside modern computational methods including deep learning and ray-tracing techniques.
